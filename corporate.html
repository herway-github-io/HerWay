Marketing

Context of campaign and promotional activities

HerWay is a community website where women can learn how to operate their campervans. It is a space where video and in-person workshops, tip and tricks, reviews and recommendations are all in one place that is easy to access for all.

Our mission is to empower and support women as they embark on their journey of self-discovery. We strive to break down barriers, challenge stereotypes, and provide the tools and resources necessary for women to embrace the camper van lifestyle on their own terms. Through a commitment to empowerment, growth, independence, and safety, we aim to create a vibrant and inclusive space by women, for women, where females can thrive, explore, and create lasting memories on the open road.

The feelings we want our users to have:

  • We want our audience to feel a sense of community with other women camper van users
  • We want our audience to feel confident and empowered in their ability to work their camper van independently and live within the camping lifestyle
  • We want our audience to feel a personal connection with the feel and voice of the brand
  • We want our audience to feel as though their knowledge and selves are growing as they continue alongside our brand

The actions we want our users to do:

  • Get to know the brand via our Instagram and website.
  • Interact with the brand Instagram and any other future promotional platforms
  • Read and contribute to articles on the website, mainly on adventures by our readers
  • Consume content about tips on starting a new journey and being on the road, also sent in by readers
  • Attend the workshops after learning about them from the workshop packages HerWay has to offer
  • Watch educational videos made by HerWay

Media Tactics

As stated above, we decided to utilize Instagram as our primary social media platform for promoting HerWay. This is because of the statistics we found that stated that it was the second most used social media platform by our target demographic (late millennial - early get z women). It also fits with the aesthetic image we want our brand to convey as well as offering us many diverse ways to post and interact with our audience.

On Instagram, our audience can find using the username @her____way. Using this account, we will be posting aesthetically pleasing images, reels and stories in order to promote our brand and increase brand awareness. These posts will consist of information about our brand and its upcoming launch to keep our audience informed, artistic quotes that match our brand values and aspirational visuals that will help curate the feel of the brand for our audience, and one on one content (interactive language like ‚Äúwhich would you pick?‚Äù and videos of our actual team members.) that will help our audience feel more connected and personally engaged with the content. On our stories we plan to repost our posts to have them pop up and be on our audiences‚Äô radar one more time if they didn‚Äôt see the post in their feed. We‚Äôll also be utilizing our stories to further our interactive and personalized content as this helps in building a stronger connection between the content creator and the audience. We want our followers to feel like they are being directly spoken to, so then they are more likely to engage with the content, comment, and share it with others (3 Reasons for Posting Personal Content on Business Social Media Accounts - Financial Resources Group Investment Services, 2023). We will accomplish this by posting things such as question boxes where our followers can leave their answers that we‚Äôll share, ‚Äúwhat kind of camper are you based on your zodiac sign?‚Äù and asking for user input and recommendations for things such as making a brand Spotify playlist, to make them feel as though they are part of the process. Finally, we want to utilize the Instagram reels feature to share videos of what our brand is about with members of our team so that our audience can feel like they are engaging with a real person rather than a faceless entity. This allows for a sense of trust and loyalty as they are more likely to trust the information shared and develop a stronger sense of loyalty towards our brand (3 Reasons for Posting Personal Content on Business Social Media Accounts - Financial Resources Group Investment Services, 2023).

Learning Points

What Worked and What Didn‚Äôt?

  • Good choice in target audience: Focusing on late millennials/early gen z‚Äôs was a smart move. Instagram, our primary social media platform thrives on visuals, high-quality images, videos, and stories. This resonates with our audience. We recognized this popularity in our own content.
  • Choice of content: We noticed that the video where we introduce ‚ÄòHerWay‚Äô and the ‚Äòphoto-dump‚Äô with aesthetically pleasing travel themed pictures were the posts with the most engagement.
  • According to our communication objectives, our goal was to gain 28 followers in three weeks. We exceeded this goal, and we are currently at a number of 96 followers.
  • Our most liked post is a video which currently has a total of 45 likes and 465 views.

What Went as Expected and What Didn‚Äôt?

  • Expected: Steady growth in followers, engagement, and brand awareness.
  • Reality: Slow start impacted our growth. However, consistent effort led to gradual improvement.

What Would You Do Differently?

  • Sole reliance on Instagram: We wanted to focus on building a brand image with aesthetic visuals which thrives best on Instagram. While Instagram is popular among this audience, it‚Äôs essential to consider other platforms too. Diversifying channels would enhance our reach. For example, Facebook is the most used social media platform for millennials. Although we didn‚Äôt use it as it didn‚Äôt match our wants for being an aesthetic and brand image, looking back, it would have been better to increase our reach in various platforms and add Facebook to our range of platforms. Being adaptable and participating in the trends our target groups follow would attract more followers.
  • Late/slow start: Additionally, we would have started the campaign sooner and started posting immediately and consistently. The brand image of ‚ÄòHerWay‚Äô would have been stronger if we had posted consistently from the beginning. We were not as active as we should have been, especially in the first two weeks where we only posted 5 times. Early planning and execution would have given us a boost and been more successful in reaching our objectives.
  • Data-Driven Decisions: If we had analyzed our metrics consistently, we could have adapted strategies based on insights. For instance, the ‚Äòphoto-dump‚Äô was most popular among our followers. Monitoring their engagement with this post would have been valuable for our interpretation. We knew that the success of the photo dump meant that it was desirable content for our target audience, but instead of creating another 'photo dump', we decided to veer towards making sure we had aesthetic content like the post had been. This was a big learning point. If we knew what it was that the followers attracted, we could have adjusted our content-calendar to prioritize similar content.

Reflect On Past Work and Describe What You Learned.

  • Overall, we learned that the marketing landscape is dynamic and changes rapidly. As a digital marketer you must be adaptable to trends and the engagement you receive. You also need patience. Building an audience takes time. Measuring your metrics along the way helps you grow and understand your audience better. The mistakes are also important to remember. The biggest mistake was not about something we did, but something we did not do. That was not posting early, consistently and solely on one platform. Involving cross-functional teams would have been valuable for our insights. Looking back, that is something we could have done differently. In the end it was a pleasant and valuable learning journey for us all, and we enjoyed making the content and celebrating our small wins along the way.

Problem

Problem

  • Problem 1: Women feel insecure about their skills and knowledge regarding camper van lifestyle.

  • Current solution: Women usually do not make this decision. Most of the time they choose to travel with their partners or a group of friends. The guides and ratings are supposed to be the most reliable form of checking if a campsite is safe for women travelling alone in all possible aspects. The aim of it is to decrease the safety concerns of women and make them feel more empowered and relaxed to go to unknown places.

Services/products

  • In the interviews we conducted with our target audience we heard a lot about various problems women can encounter as female camper van users, such as: safety concerns connected to the environment being dominated by men but also connected to lack of technical knowledge. Women felt very uncomfortable travelling alone due to insecurities connected to vehicle maintenance, technical issues and concerns about safety of campsites.
  • HerWay solves those problems in multiple ways providing the target audience with various solutions tailored to their needs. The products/services we provide as solutions include:
    • Online video tutorials on various subjects such as: Van makeover, Tips and Tricks, DIY
    • Physical workshops on topics of: safety, technical issues, vehicle maintenance or self defense
    • Articles with rankings of camping sites regarding safety for women
  • Our value proposition talks mostly about empowerment of women to travel alone with a camper van. However, the first step to be empowered is to feel confident and secure. With our products we aim to provide our audience with those feelings and place them on the right path to reach full empowerment at the end. By providing women with those tutorials and workshops we want to make them feel more comfortable in their skills and knowledge what will allow them to travel alone in a more comfortable and confident way.
  • All of our products/services are placed or accessible through our website. Since our vision is focused on creating a perfect sanctuary for women willing to grow their mental and physical skills it connects perfectly to the idea of a website as the sanctuary. The website is a great representation what our brand stands for.

Justification problem interviews

  • We got many insights from our problem interviews. We initially focused on community building as a primary issue whilethe real challenges for women travelers focused on safety, knowledge gaps, and a lack of empowerment. This was crucial in reshaping our whole brand idea and identity since it completely changed all the problems and solutions. Without the interviews we would still be focusing only on the community problem that came out to be non-existent. Our value proposition was also completely changed after those discoveries. Even though we got a lot of different problems, we had to focus only on some of them to make our brand as tailored to our target audience as possible.

Validation of Assumptions

The first lean canvas was made completely on our assumptions. We didn‚Äôt have any ideas on who to choose for our customer segments and therefore, we chose to target all the camper van users we can access without any specifications. This shifted very quickly after a few of the first problem interviews and then we decided to focus strictly on female millennials seeking guidance on starting their solo travelling journey with a camper van. It was caused by the most insights in this topic during the interviews with both men and women.

The same happened with our assumed problem about community. We thought that building and keeping the community within the camper van environment would be hard. This assumption was made based on researching the Facebook groups and lack of activity on most of them. We saw a lot of people writing posts but not so many replying to them. However, from all participants asked about this issue only one confirmed it, the rest didn‚Äôt find it a problem. Therefore, we switched our focus to other problems that our more specified customer segment struggled with.

Together with changing the problem we obviously were required to change the solutions and therefore our value proposition. The first value proposition stated that we want to create a community, the second one included only empowerment of women and the third one put the way to reach this empowerment as well what is crucial for our brand. Without specific resources, one will never be able to reach the final goal of independence and empowerment. Therefore, it can be concluded that most of our initial assumptions were wrong and the interviews provided us with a lot of valuable insights enabling us to create a better brand and product.
